the REQUIEM survey

Quasars at z∼6 are among the brightest sources in the Universe. The mass of their central black holes grows by 20 times in only 100 Myr via gas accretion and their host galaxies form stars with hundreds of solar masses per year. How can these extreme sources sustain such a rapid gas consumption? An answer to this question may come from our ongoing VLT/MUSE effort targeting z>5.7 quasars: Reionization Epoch QUasar InvEstigation with MUSE (REQUIEM) survey.

Temperature map of the gas in a 1012.5M☉ dark matter halo at z∼6 (courtesy of Dr. Tiago Costa).

The main goal of the REQUIEM survey is to probe the complex interplay between the first quasars and their surroundings. The early formation of super-massi Cool (T∼104K) accretion streams are able to survive the violent shocks induced during the first phases of a super-massive black hole ignition. With our VLT/MUSE iniziative we are able to trace this fundamental ingredient for the formation of the first quasars via its Hydrogen Lyα emission.
